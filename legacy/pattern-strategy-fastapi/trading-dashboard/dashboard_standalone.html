Patterns Traded

Double Top (DT) — Sell setup

Enter at R2 after price has tagged the same resistance twice. This is where most retail traders are taking profit on longs and waiting for a neckline break — you're selling the second touch instead.

R1 R2 ← entry here (sell) /\ /\ / \ / \ / \ / \ / \/ \ ← neckline / \

Stop loss: just above R1/R2 zone.
Take profit: 1:2 minimum, hold for 1:3+ when momentum is one-sided.

90% historical reversal rate at R2 if both confirmations fire.

Double Bottom (DB) — Buy setup

Mirror of Double Top. Enter at R2 after price has tagged the same support twice.

\ / \ / \ /\ / \ / \ / \/ \/ B1 B2 ← entry here (buy)

Stop loss: just below B1/B2 zone.
Take profit: minimum 1:2.

Head & Shoulders (HS) — Sell setup

"Premature H&S" entry — sell at the top of the right shoulder instead of waiting for the neckline break + retest.

H /\ / \ / \ LS / \ RS ← entry here (sell) /\ / \ /\ / v v \ ---------------- neckline

Wait for momentum + candle confirmation at the right shoulder before pulling the trigger.

Inverse Head & Shoulders (IHS) — Buy setup

Mirror of H&S. Buy at the bottom of the right shoulder, before neckline break.

Rectangle — Breakout setup

Range-bound consolidation. Enter on the breakout candle close, not on a retest — momentum tends to continue when the break is clean.

___________________ resistance | | | range | ← wait for clean break |___________________| support \ ← breakout candle close

Trendline Break

Enter on a clean break + close of a trendline drawn across at least 3 touches. Combined with the candle confirmation rules for sizing.

Confirmation Requirements

Momentum Confirmation

When price returns to the zone (R2 / shoulder / range edge), the approach must be weaker than the prior leg.

  • ✓
    Slow, choppy approach into R2
    Multiple small-to-medium candles, not one violent move
  • ✗
    Reject: explosive 1–2 candle approach
    Indicates the dominant side is too strong — skip the trade
  • ~
    3-bar avg range ≤ 2.5× ATR(14)
    Quantifiable proxy if you're scoring trades programmatically

Candle Confirmation

Opposite-color candle at the zone (red at DT/HS, green at DB/IHS).

  • ●
    Medium body, not jumbo
    A massive candle often = exhaustion; 90% chance of reversal after
  • ●
    Full-body close
    Body ≥ 70% of total range
  • ●
    Opposing wick ≤ 30%
    Lower wick on a sell candle, upper wick on a buy candle
  • ●
    Total wicks ≤ 30%
    Clean directional close, not a doji or pin
Risk Management Rules
  • 1%
    1% risk per trade — always
    Applies equally to real accounts and prop-firm funded accounts. No exceptions for "high-conviction" setups.
  • 1:2
    Minimum R:R of 1:2
    Every trade must be sized so SL → TP distance is at least 2× the entry → SL distance.
  • SL
    Stop loss above/below the pattern zone
    Above R1/R2 for shorts, below B1/B2 for longs. Not "X pips" — structurally placed.
  • 10–12
    10–12 trades per week is the target
    Across all instruments combined. If you're hitting 20+, you're forcing trades.
  • ∅
    No 1-minute / 5-minute scalping
    15m and above. Lower TFs produce noise that breaks the candle confirmation rule.
Trade Management — When to Exit
  • A
    Counter-pattern forms before 1:2 → cut or move to break-even
    If you're short on a DT and a Double Bottom forms before TP, exit. 90% of the time this kind of reversal pattern follows through.
  • B
    High-impact news → move to BE or close if in profit
    CPI, FOMC, NFP — these events invalidate the chart context. Don't hold full risk through them.
  • C
    Otherwise: don't touch the trade
    No early take-profits, no "feeling-based" exits. Let SL or TP do their job.

Three trade categories

Every signal falls into one of these three buckets. Adherence is scored differently for each.

RED FLAG Half-formed pattern, pending confirmation

R1 done, R2 forming. Pre-mapped on chart. Wait for price to revisit zone AND a confirmation candle to close before entering.

LIVE Pattern complete, candle confirmed

Given with SL and TP in real time during the live session. Highest-volume bucket.

TELEGRAM Premium-quality, post-confirmation only

Pushed only after the confirmation candle closes. Highest historical accuracy of the three buckets.

Skip the trade if…
  • ✗
    Approach to zone is explosive
    Wide candles, gap-like moves
  • ✗
    Confirmation candle is too large
    Often exhaustion — high reversal odds
  • ✗
    Confirmation candle is too small / doji
    No directional conviction
  • ✗
    High-impact news inside 2 hours
    CPI / FOMC / NFP / central bank decisions

Data You Should Add Next

The dashboard already covers the basics, but trading-agent decision quality compounds with richer telemetry. Here are the high-leverage additions, ranked by signal-per-effort.

1. Entry-context candle snapshots

Save the ~50 candles around each entry. Lets you re-score adherence as your rules evolve, and trains a future ML classifier for "is this a clean confirmation candle?"

2. Slippage & fill quality

Requested price vs actual fill, in pips. Reveals broker issues and lets you separate strategy edge from execution decay.

3. Time-of-day & session breakdown

Tag each trade with Asia / London / NY session. Most price-action setups have a session preference — surface it.

4. Economic-event proximity

For each trade, log the nearest CPI/FOMC/NFP/CB-decision and minutes to/from it. Validates the news-exit rule with numbers.

5. ATR + spread at entry

Save ATR(14) and average spread at the moment of entry. Lets you size R correctly and detect setups where spread ate the R:R.

6. Higher-TF context tag

For every M15/H1 trade, record the H4 trend direction. Adherence should drop for counter-HTF trades.

7. Adherence-vs-PnL scatter

X-axis: adherence score. Y-axis: R-multiple outcome. The hypothesis is a positive correlation — visualise it and you'll know if the rules are actually edge.

8. Max Adverse / Favourable Excursion (MAE / MFE)

Per trade, the worst drawdown and best run-up before close. MAE tells you if SLs are too tight; MFE tells you if TPs are leaving money on the table.

9. Trade-cap discipline tracker

Trades #1–12 of the week have one win rate; trades #13+ usually have a much worse one. Plot the cliff to prove (or disprove) the 10–12/week ceiling.

10. Replay viewer per trade

Click any trade in the log → see candles N bars before & after entry, with SL/TP/entry lines overlaid. Cheap to build, huge for journaling.

11. Open-position heat map

Current exposure by currency (USD long, GBP short, etc.) — flags accidental correlated bets.

12. Streak detector

Win streaks → emotional tilt risk. Loss streaks → strategy drift risk. Trigger a "pause and review" banner after N consecutive of either.

For the auto-trading agent
  • A
    Half-position size at first, every new symbol
    Until you have ≥ 30 trades on that symbol with positive expectancy.
  • B
    Hard daily-loss circuit breaker
    2× the per-trade risk (2%) — stop trading for the day if hit.
  • C
    Pattern detector should propose, not auto-execute
    Phase 1: alert only. Phase 2: paper trade. Phase 3: live with a kill switch.
  • D
    Adherence score gate
    Auto-skip trades scoring < 80% on the rule checklist.
  • E
    News blackout window
    No new entries within 30 minutes of any "high" impact event on the symbol's currencies.
